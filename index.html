Fields of interest:
  • Macroeconomics;
  • Time series econometrics;
  • Banking.

I use empirical methods to study monetary policy, financial stability, and the banking sector. My work identifies common and idiosyncratic factors driving bank-level behavior with the goal of estimating the distributional effects of macroeconomic policies and assessing structural developments in the financial system. I also develop time series econometric and computational methods to improve the macroeconomist's toolkit.

In tandem with my research, I develop statistical/computational libraries for R and Julia users (available on GitHub).
